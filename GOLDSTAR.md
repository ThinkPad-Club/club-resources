# Gold Star Students ⭐️

At the beginning of each semester, everyone in ThinkPad Club starts as a Gold Star Student. To stay a Gold Star Student, students simply need to follow the Class Conduct rules, try their best during coding demonstrations, and make an effort to complete the take-home assignments. Gold Star Students help others learn by focusing on their own work and avoiding behavior that distracts other students. After our daily coding demonstration, students continue working on the project from the demo, ask questions, and keep practicing their coding skills. Students who showed Gold Star behavior during the previous class session will be able to choose special elective activities that only one student can use at a time, such as LEGO SPIKE robotics kits, Game Boy game development, building circuit boards, disassembling old electronics, working on a personal website on the class server, experimenting with electronic music equipment, and other activities as they become available. Because these materials are limited and sometimes delicate, they require extra trust and responsibility. At the start of class, the names of Gold Star Students from the previous session will be written on the board in a random order, and students will choose their activity in that order to keep things fair.

## How to Be a Gold Star Student
- You had excellent behavior during the previous class session  
- You tried your best to follow along with the code demonstration  
- You made an effort to complete the take-home activities  
- You continue to behave responsibly during class  

## What Gold Star Students Get
- The chance to choose an elective project or activity after the code demonstration the next day  
- A Pokémon mini-figure at the end of class  
- A random Pokémon card at the end of class  
- The pride of knowing they did their best
